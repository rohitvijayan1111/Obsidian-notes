# Marubozu

### **Chapter 5.2 ‚Äì The Marubozu**

- **Definition**: "Marubozu" means ‚ÄúBald‚Äù in Japanese; it‚Äôs a single candlestick pattern with no upper or lower shadow (i.e., no wicks).
    
- **Types**:
    
    1. **Bullish Marubozu** ‚Äì Indicates strong buying pressure.
        
    2. **Bearish Marubozu** ‚Äì Indicates strong selling pressure.
        
- **Key Point**: Marubozu **violates Rule 3** of candlestick trading (i.e., _"look for prior trend"_). It can appear anywhere on the chart and still be valid.
    

---

### **Three Important Candlestick Rules (Recap)**:

1. **Buy strength, sell weakness**
    
2. **Be flexible, verify and quantify patterns**
    
3. **Look for prior trend** _(violated by Marubozu)_
    

---

### **Chapter 5.3 ‚Äì Bullish Marubozu**

- **OHLC Criteria**:
    
    - **Open = Low**
        
    - **High = Close**
        
- **Indicates**: Intense buying throughout the session. Sentiment has turned bullish.
    
- **Trading Strategy**:
    
    - **Buy** around the **closing price**.
        
    - **Stoploss** at the **low** of the marubozu.
        

#### **Risk Profiles**:

- **Risk Taker**:
    
    - Buys **on the same day**, near 3:20 PM after validating:
        
        - Open ‚âà Low
            
        - CMP ‚âà High
            
- **Risk Averse**:
    
    - Buys **next day** only if it's a **blue (bullish) candle**.
        
    - Confirmation before entering.
        
    - Disadvantage: higher entry, deeper stoploss.
        

#### **Real Example**:

- Stock: **ACC Limited**
    
- OHLC: O = 971.8, H = 1030.2, L = 970.1, C = 1028.4
    
- Trade Setup:
    
    - **Buy** @ 1028.4
        
    - **Stoploss** @ 970.0
        

---

### **Chapter 5.4 ‚Äì Stoploss in Bullish Marubozu**

- **Built-in Risk Management**: Stoploss = **Low** of the Marubozu.
    
- **If stoploss is hit**:
    
    - Exit the trade.
        
    - Accept the loss.
        
    - Do **not deviate** from trading rules.
        

---

### **Chapter 5.3 ‚Äì Bearish Marubozu**

- **OHLC Criteria**:
    
    - **Open = High**
        
    - **Close = Low**
        
- **Indicates**: Intense selling throughout the session. Sentiment has turned bearish.
    
- **Trading Strategy**:
    
    - **Sell** (short) around the **closing price**.
        
    - **Stoploss** at the **high** of the marubozu.
        

#### **Risk Profiles**:

- **Risk Taker**:
    
    - Shorts on the same day after confirming:
        
        - Open ‚âà High
            
        - CMP ‚âà Low
            
- **Risk Averse**:
    
    - Shorts the **next day** only if it‚Äôs a **red (bearish) candle**.
        
    - Ensures strength in bearishness.
        

#### **Real Example**:

- Stock: **BPCL**
    
- OHLC: O = 355.4, H = 356.0, L = 341.0, C = 341.7
    
- Trade Setup:
    
    - **Short** @ 341.7
        
    - **Stoploss** @ 356.0
        

---

### **Chapter 5.4 ‚Äì Trade Trap: Avoid Extreme Candles**

- **Avoid** candles with:
    
    - **Range < 1%** (Too small ‚Üí No clear trend).
        
    - **Range > 10%** (Too big ‚Üí Stoploss too deep).
        
- **Reason**:
    
    - Small candles = low activity ‚Üí weak signal.
        
    - Long candles = high volatility ‚Üí risky stoploss.
        

---

### **Summary of Marubozu Trade Rules**

|Type|Entry Condition|Stoploss Placement|
|---|---|---|
|**Bullish Marubozu**|Buy near close (CMP ‚âà High, Open ‚âà Low)|Low of the candle|
|**Bearish Marubozu**|Sell near close (CMP ‚âà Low, Open ‚âà High)|High of the candle|
|**Risk Taker**|Acts same day if criteria met|Close to real-time|
|**Risk Averse**|Acts next day only if same trend continues|Safer, but deeper SL|

# üìò Chapter 6: Spinning Tops & Dojis ‚Äî Indecision Candles

---

## üåÄ 6.1 ‚Äì **Spinning Tops**

### üîç **What is a Spinning Top?**

A **spinning top** is a type of candlestick with:

- A **small real body** (open and close are close together)
    
- A **long upper shadow**
    
- A **long lower shadow**
    
- Shadows (wicks) are **almost equal**
    

**Color doesn't matter** ‚Äî it could be red or blue.

---

### üîÑ **What Does It Represent?**

It shows **indecision in the market**.

#### üí° Think of the candle as a story:

1. **Small Body** ‚Üí Bulls and bears fought all day but closed the day with almost no gain or loss.
    
2. **Upper Wick** ‚Üí Bulls tried to take the price up but failed.
    
3. **Lower Wick** ‚Üí Bears tried to push it down but also failed.
    

> **Conclusion**: No side (bulls or bears) could win. Market is confused. It's a **"pause" candle**, not a signal by itself.

---

## üìâ 6.2 ‚Äì **Spinning Tops in a Downtrend**

When a spinning top appears **after a continuous fall**, here‚Äôs what it could mean:

### üß† Interpretations:

1. **Bears might be tired** ‚Äì They pushed the price down but now are losing steam.
    
2. **Bulls are trying to fight back** ‚Äì They‚Äôre entering but haven‚Äôt won yet.
    

### ü§î What can happen next?

There are **two equal possibilities**:

- üìâ The **downtrend continues** if bears regain control.
    
- üìà A **trend reversal** starts if bulls succeed.
    

---

### üõ†Ô∏è **Trading Strategy in a Downtrend**

Because it‚Äôs unclear, play **defensively**:

- If you want to **go long (buy)**:
    
    - Buy only **half the quantity** (e.g., 250 shares if your full quantity is 500).
        
    - Wait for confirmation (if price rises the next day).
        
    - If it rises ‚Üí **buy more** (average up).
        
    - If it falls ‚Üí **exit** early with **limited loss** on a smaller position.
        

üß† _This approach reduces your risk while keeping you ready to benefit if the reversal actually happens._

---

## üìà 6.2 ‚Äì **Spinning Tops in an Uptrend**

Now imagine the **market has been going up** for several days, and then a spinning top appears.

### üß† Interpretations:

1. **Bulls might be tired** ‚Äì They‚Äôve run out of strength.
    
2. **Bears are testing** ‚Äì They‚Äôre trying to push down.
    

Again, this shows **uncertainty**.

---

### üõ†Ô∏è **Trading Strategy in an Uptrend**

If you‚Äôre **already holding** the stock:

- **Book profit on 50%** of your position.
    
- Keep the other 50% invested.
    

#### What can happen next?

- üìâ If price drops ‚Üí you‚Äôve locked in gains on half, and you can sell the rest.
    
- üìà If price rises ‚Üí you‚Äôre still in the game with 50% and can enjoy more gains.
    

üß† _This method lets you protect profits while not missing future upside._

---

## üßò 6.3 ‚Äì **The Doji Candle**

### üîç What is a Doji?

A **doji** is a candle with:

- **Open = Close** (or nearly the same)
    
- **Very small or no real body**
    
- Can have **long or short shadows**
    

> It looks like a ‚Äú+‚Äù or a ‚Äúcross‚Äù.

---

### üîÅ Doji vs. Spinning Top:

|Feature|Spinning Top|Doji|
|---|---|---|
|Real Body|Small but visible|Very tiny or none|
|Meaning|Indecision|Indecision|
|Appearance|Top with wicks above & below|Looks like a cross or plus|

üß† _In practice, they are both treated similarly._

---

## üîÄ **What Do These Candles Mean for You?**

Whenever you see a **spinning top** or **doji** ‚Äî especially after a clear trend ‚Äî it means:

- The current trend may be **weakening**
    
- A **reversal or continuation** is equally possible
    
- **Big movement is coming**, but direction is uncertain
    

Think of them as:

> üîï **"Pause" candles** that warn of a potential storm ahead ‚Äî not trade signals by themselves.

---

## üìä Example: How to Act Based on These Candles

### üéØ Case 1 ‚Äì Downtrend + Spinning Top

You see a stock falling for 5 days. On Day 6, a spinning top appears.

‚Üí You think, ‚ÄúMaybe the fall is ending.‚Äù

**What you do:**

- Buy **half quantity**
    
- Set a **tight stoploss** below the spinning top‚Äôs low
    
- Wait for Day 7:
    
    - If green candle = buy more (add)
        
    - If red candle = exit (cut losses early)
        

---

### üéØ Case 2 ‚Äì Uptrend + Doji

You bought a stock at ‚Çπ200. It rallied to ‚Çπ250 over a week. Suddenly, you see a **doji** on the chart.

‚Üí You think, ‚ÄúMarket is pausing. Something‚Äôs up.‚Äù

**What you do:**

- Sell **half** your holding
    
- Wait:
    
    - If market falls ‚Üí Sell rest, book full profit
        
    - If market rises ‚Üí Enjoy profits on remaining shares
        

---

## üìù Key Takeaways

1. **Spinning tops** have small bodies, equal upper & lower shadows. They show **indecision**.
    
2. **Color doesn't matter** ‚Äì it‚Äôs the structure that‚Äôs important.
    
3. They appear at **tops and bottoms** of trends.
    
4. They don‚Äôt give clear buy/sell signals ‚Äî but **warn you** of change.
    
5. In uncertain situations:
    
    - **Reduce risk** by taking half trades or booking partial profits.
        
    - **Wait for confirmation** (next day‚Äôs candle direction).
        
6. **Dojis** are like spinning tops with **no body at all**. Treated similarly.
    

---

## üéì Final Tip:

Always **combine** spinning tops and dojis with:

- Volume analysis
    
- Trendlines
    
- Support/resistance zones
    
- Next day's candle for confirmation
    

That way, you avoid **false signals** and **trade with confidence**.

# Hanging man -> REVERSAL PATTERN

You're doing great keeping up with the candlestick learning journey. Let's now break down **Chapter 7.3 ‚Äì The Hanging Man** in a **very clear, structured, and practical** way. We'll also compare it with the Hammer where necessary to build deeper insight.

---

## üìç What is a Hanging Man?

- A **Hanging Man** is a **single candlestick pattern**.
    
- It is **bearish** and suggests a **possible reversal from an uptrend to a downtrend**.
    
- It is **exactly the same candle structure** as the **Hammer** ‚Äî the difference is **where** it appears.
    

> üü© **Hammer** = Appears **at the bottom of a downtrend** ‚Üí **Bullish reversal**  
> üü• **Hanging Man** = Appears **at the top of an uptrend** ‚Üí **Bearish reversal**

---

## üïµÔ∏è‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è Candle Structure (Technical Criteria)

For a candle to qualify as a **Hanging Man (paper umbrella)**:

|Part|Description|
|---|---|
|**Real Body**|Small body near the **top of the range**|
|**Lower Shadow**|Must be at least **2x the body** in length|
|**Upper Shadow**|Very small or none|
|**Color**|Doesn't matter ‚Äî red or green is okay|
|**Trend Before**|Must be in an **uptrend**|

---

## üß† Psychological Meaning Behind the Pattern

Let‚Äôs interpret what happens _inside_ the market when a Hanging Man forms:

1. üêÇ The **bulls** are in control; price is rising with higher highs.
    
2. üêª On the hanging man day, **price drops sharply during the session** (long lower shadow).
    
3. üêÇ But by the end, bulls **pull the price back up**, closing near the top.
    
4. ‚ùó But this drop shows **weakness** ‚Äî it means **bears entered** and tried to pull prices down.
    
5. Even though bulls recovered, the presence of sellers creates a **warning sign**.
    

So it‚Äôs **not a strong sell signal by itself**. It's a **"potential top"** ‚Äî a red flag that **bulls may be losing control**.

---

## üìâ Trading Strategy with the Hanging Man

### üéØ Objective: **Short Trade Setup (Sell first, buy later)**

### üßçFor the **Risk-Taker**:

- **Enter**: Short **on the same day** as the hanging man candle, near the **close price** (around 3:25 PM).
    
- **Stoploss**: Place **above the high** of the hanging man candle.
    
- **Example**:
    
    - OHLC: Open = ‚Çπ592, High = ‚Çπ593.75, Low = ‚Çπ587, Close = ‚Çπ593
        
    - Short at: ‚Çπ593
        
    - Stoploss: ‚Çπ594 (above the high)
        

### üßç‚Äç‚ôÇÔ∏èFor the **Risk-Averse Trader**:

- **Wait for the next day**:
    
    - If the **next candle is red (bearish)**, it confirms the downtrend may start.
        
- **Enter**: Short near the **close** of that red candle (e.g. at 3:25 PM).
    
- **Stoploss**: Same ‚Äî high of the hanging man candle.

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Limitations of the Hanging Man

This is important!

> ‚ùó Even though bears were able to push prices down during the day, **bulls recovered** by the close ‚Äî so the trend **hasn't reversed yet**.

This is why the author prefers the **Hammer** more than the Hanging Man.

> "If the bears were so strong, why couldn‚Äôt they close the price near the low?"

So, it‚Äôs best to:

- **Wait for confirmation** (next day should be bearish)
    
- Use it as an **early warning**, not a full signal
    
- Combine with volume, resistance zone, or RSI divergence for more accuracy
